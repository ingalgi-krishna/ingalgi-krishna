## About

I am **Krishna Ingalgi**, Founder and Chief Operating Officer of **Sucetas Technologies** — a custom software and AI development company I co-founded in September 2024 and bootstrapped to production deployments across manufacturing, education, retail, and e-commerce.

I run two things day to day: **operations** for the services business in Pune, and **product** for our SaaS line under the UK entity. In practice that means I am equally in the roadmap and in the codebase. I have personally shipped our e-commerce platform, all three company websites, ongoing product features, and — solo — our AI voice agent system.

My work sits where **applied AI** meets **things that have to survive contact with a real factory floor**: computer vision on production lines, retrieval-augmented conversational agents, real-time voice, and the operations discipline to keep them running once the demo is over.

> Good technology should feel invisible. It just works, and makes life a little bit better.

**Focus areas:** AI product development · Computer vision · Conversational and voice AI · SaaS architecture · Full-stack engineering (Next.js, FastAPI, PostgreSQL) · Cloud (AWS, GCP) · Startup operations and go-to-market

## Client work under NDA

Two of the systems I am proudest of belong to clients and stay anonymous. The outcomes are published; the names are not.

<details>
<summary><b>Injection-molding production intelligence</b> — computer vision on the factory floor</summary>
<br>

Production monitoring for injection molding plants using **one IP camera per machine** and a single shared edge device per plant. No machine modification, no retrofitted sensors, and fully compatible with legacy equipment that predates the idea of an API.

It detects blank shots, classifies downtime by cause (power, fault, quality), catches slow cycles, and runs through the night on infrared. Role-based dashboards give the floor, the supervisor, and the owner different views of the same truth.

**Better than 98% accuracy** — where sensor-based approaches cap out below 95%.
**+15% efficiency · −22% idle time**

<sub>Computer vision · Edge inference · Real-time dashboards</sub>

</details>

<details>
<summary><b>Vision-based HRMS</b> — face-recognition attendance and payroll</summary>
<br>

A full HR system built around a custom face-recognition engine: attendance without cards or fingerprints, dual shifts with rotation, hourly payroll, advance tracking, and audit logs. Offline-first and on-premise, because the factories that need it most cannot depend on the internet.

**Better than 99% attendance accuracy · −80% processing time · +95% accuracy**

<sub>FastAPI · Next.js · TypeScript · PostgreSQL · Custom face recognition · WebSockets</sub>

</details>

<summary><b>How I work</b></summary>
<br>

I do not think the founder-who-codes thing is a novelty; it is the only way I know to keep the roadmap honest. If I cannot build it, I should not be promising it.

Most of what I do is closing the distance between a strategy deck and something running in production at 2am in a factory with bad wifi. That means I care disproportionately about the unglamorous parts: onboarding, error states, offline behaviour, and what happens on day 200 rather than day 1.

I bootstrap. Sucetas has taken no outside capital and reached production deployments on revenue, which shapes everything about how we pick problems.
