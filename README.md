## About

I am **Krishna Ingalgi**, Founder and Chief Operating Officer of **Sucetas Technologies**, a custom software and AI company I co-founded in September 2024 and grew to production deployments without outside capital.

I lead operations and product. Across our first two years I have been responsible for our e-commerce platform, our company websites, the Dunefox product cycle, and our AI voice agent system, working alongside the engineers who built them with me. The part of the role I protect is the building itself. Most weeks I am in the roadmap and in the codebase on the same day, and I would not know how to run product any other way.

What I care about is AI that survives contact with real users, and the product judgement to know which problems are worth solving at all. If I cannot build it, I should not be promising it to a customer.

> Good technology should feel invisible. It just works, and makes life a little bit better.

**Focus:** AI product development, conversational and voice AI, SaaS architecture, full-stack engineering (Next.js, FastAPI, PostgreSQL), cloud (AWS, GCP), operations and go-to-market.

Alongside the products, our engineering team ships systems for clients under NDA. **Production intelligence** watches injection molding machines through a single camera each, with nothing bolted onto the machine, and reads what they are doing to better than **99% accuracy**. **Vision-based attendance and payroll** replaces cards and fingerprints with a camera that recognises faces, and runs without an internet connection. <summary><b>How I work</b></summary>
<br>

I do not think a founder who codes is a novelty. It is the only way I know to keep a roadmap honest.

Most of my job is closing the distance between a strategy deck and something running in production for a customer who does not care how clever it is. That means I care disproportionately about the unglamorous parts: onboarding, error states, and what happens on day 200 rather than day 1.

We bootstrap. Sucetas has taken no outside capital and reached production deployments on revenue, which shapes everything about how we choose problems.
